# AB Dynamics consultancy — "Networking & Introductions" event (Mon 08/06)

Kickoff event for the AB Dynamics consultancy project (scoped to data analysis / pipeline). Bio page + 10 questions for the AB people.

## Bio page

**Why this project?**
It was allocated rather than chosen, but is a useful fit for developing the data-driven, decision-rationale side of my engineering.

**What I hope to get out of this project**
- Real industry consultancy experience with a real client and real constraints.
- Get better at evidencing the *why* behind design decisions, not just the *what*.
- How engineering decisions get made and justified in a larger, non-SME R&D setting.
- Practice the client-facing side: requirements, communication, delivery.

**Key interests**
- R&D engineering: taking novel concepts from idea to working hardware.
- Robotics and autonomy, especially manipulation and full-stack integration.
- Custom actuator and reducer design.
- Space robotics and in-space assembly: autonomy, coordination, and manipulation in extreme, human-inaccessible environments.

**Key strengths**
- Full-stack mechatronics: paper to working hardware.
- Systems-architect mindset with a hardware bias.
- Methodical hardware testing and characterisation.
- Practical delivery: turning ambiguous problems into working prototypes.

**Areas for improvement**
- Making my decision rationale explicit: the *why* behind designs.
- People and team coordination and management.
- Communicating to mixed or non-specialist audiences.
- Deepening data-analysis and statistical rigour.

## 10 questions for AB

*Arc: current state → the data → what good looks like → the deliverable.*

1. How is the system currently tuned, and who does it — manual, expert-driven, semi-automated?
2. What's the main limitation of the current approach that prompted this project — time, cost, consistency, scalability?
3. What data is available, in what form, and how is it collected and stored — is there an existing pipeline, or does one need building?
4. How clean and well-labelled is the data, and are there known gaps or quality issues to expect?
5. Is the data from physical test rigs, simulation, or both, and how well do they agree?
6. How do the different data sources relate to each other — a unified view, or siloed across systems?
7. What does "well-tuned" actually mean to you — what metrics define a good outcome? *(the key one: it's the decision-rationale thread the project is really about)*
8. How much does the optimal tuning vary across vehicles, setups, or conditions — one general solution, or per-configuration?
9. Are there constraints on the approach — does it need to be interpretable to operators, real-time, or safety-bounded?
10. What would success look like at the end — a tool, a methodology, a proof-of-concept? And who are the end users?
