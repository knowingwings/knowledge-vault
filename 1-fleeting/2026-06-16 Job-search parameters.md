# Job-search parameters (decide before the pressure, not during it)

Reusable across every application/offer (Lunasa, Lodestar, all future). Pin the negotiables vs non-negotiables here so each offer is a check-against-criteria, not a fresh agonise. This is itself a silent-plans-tax payment.

## Salary
- **Target: £40-45k** — confident, well-justified by the portfolio (top-of-graduate-band). Aim the conversation here.
- **Stated floor (Tom's call, 16/06): £40k** — held as a deliberate choice, *knowing* it means walking from sub-£40k offers even at a preferred ISAM company.
- **2-year trajectory: £50-60k** (London mid-level, 2-4 yrs professional) — NOT a first-offer number; the direction, not the ask.
- **True livability floor (London): ~£32-35k gross** — below this a London role goes backwards regardless of how good it is.

**Market reality (UK 2026, verified 16/06):** graduate robotics/space band £28-40k (median £28-35k); London blended averages £47-54k are *all-experience*, not grad (25th pct ~£36k); comparable London space startup (Lodestar) E1 Junior band £30-55k. So £40k = top-of-junior, achievable, NOT a baseline.

**Honest position calibration:** upper-end JUNIOR (strong portfolio = low-risk high-ceiling grad), not entering at mid-level (that's earned in 1-2 yrs, likely fast). Negotiate confidently from the portfolio; reach mid-level comp by proving it, don't enter on it.

**Myth-checks (so negotiation rests on fact):** (a) inflation has *not* made £40k the universal entry baseline — it's above median; (b) startups generally pay *less* base than primes/big-tech (trade cash for equity/responsibility/learning) — the exceptions are well-funded defence/AI (Helsing-likes). Target startups for breadth + equity + responsibility, not base pay.

**Strategic overlay (founding brief):** the ISAM job is the apprenticeship/runway phase — comp is secondary to network, skills, sector exposure. For a *preferred* ISAM target, weight learning/equity/mission over base; don't let a worth-based floor cost the apprenticeship.

**Negotiating position (16/06):** interim fallback = home in Guernsey with parents (zero rent) until a job lands. So there's **no income pressure forcing a quick/wrong acceptance** — the floor is genuinely affordable to hold, not bravado, and the search can wait for the right role. Use this as quiet leverage; never disclose it.

## Non-salary must-haves / wants
- **Private medical — effectively a MUST (load-bearing):** the AXA/ADHD route was closed (12/06) *on the basis* that employer private medical covers the ADHD assessment. So "private medical that covers ADHD assessment" is a strategic must-have, not a perk. **Check coverage at offer stage.**
- **Pension matching** — want (good-to-have).
- **Equity** — relevant at startups (Lunasa now Infinite-Orbits-backed); part of total comp, weigh it in.

## Location
- **Flexible — no constraint.** Opens UK + EU (Toulouse/Infinite Orbits, the whole founding-brief European apprenticeship angle). A genuine asset in negotiation and targeting.

## Values line (defence)
- **Will take defence roles**, EXCEPT those supporting regimes Tom objects to (explicit example: Israel). Decide per-role consciously (founding-brief rule: don't drift into defence-primary by accident). Helsing-type already deemed acceptable.

## How to use
- **Screening calls:** don't raise salary; if they raise it, state the range (£40-45k target / open on the right package) and move on. Negotiate at offer stage with leverage.
- **Offer stage:** check against this list — salary vs target/floor, private-medical+ADHD coverage, pension, equity, values. Total package, not just base.
