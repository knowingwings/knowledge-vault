3. **The "easy out" peer-review catch from Dom.** I'd over-corrected from v1's too-aggressive "not keen to work in" framing to v2's too-soft "commit fully... opportunity to broaden" framing. Dom caught that the over-correction gave faculty an easy exit. A second pair of eyes is the cheapest insurance against this.**


### What confused me? (Valuable information, not failure)

**The tension between asking for what I want and accepting reality.** The email had to express *both*: "I'd prefer iNetic / individual project" AND "I understand if those aren't possible." Calibrating that without contradicting myself was harder than the strategic decision itself. Drafted three versions:
- **v1 (mine):** *"a domain I am not overly familiar nor keen to work in"* — too aggressive, read as coded threat
- **v2 (revised):** *"commit fully... approach it as an opportunity to broaden"* — too soft, gave easy out
- **Final (cut):** removed the trade-off acknowledgment entirely. The closing "Happy to discuss further" carries the openness without pre-accepting.

The lesson: sometimes the right move with a problematic paragraph isn't to fix it — it's to ask whether it should exist at all. Often the surrounding text already does the work.
