## Daily Reflection (22:00, 5 min)

### What did I learn today that I didn't know yesterday?

**The phone-reach habit is deeper than just dopamine-craving — it's a drift-from-utility pattern.** Pulling out the phone for something legitimate (calc, camera, responding to messages, navigation) repeatedly drifts into Instagram / Twitter / scroll-something. The hook isn't always "I want to scroll" — it's "I have the phone open already, the scroll-app is one swipe away." Different mechanism than pure craving; same outcome.

Hope for this week: the detox window either breaks this habit fully, or at minimum I get better at noticing the drift moment and stopping it. Already visible today on the Twitter-via-notifications micro-slips (~30 sec stops). If the noticing-and-stopping skill compounds across the week, that's a real win regardless of whether the underlying urge changes.

**Implication worth holding:** if drift-from-utility is the main mechanism, a structural fix beyond detox might be: route utility tasks off the phone where possible (desktop for messages, dedicated kitchen calc, separate camera). Phone-in-hand reduction, not just app-removal. Don't act on this yet — let the detox run first, see what the result actually looks like.


### What confused me? (Valuable information, not failure)

**How did I develop this habit?** Four years ago I was staunchly against short-form media — TikTok, Reels were the things I actively disliked and avoided. Today I've completely fallen victim to them. Stark trajectory shift; don't have a clean account of when or why the prior dropped.

Asymmetric pattern worth noting: **Reddit doomscroll I kicked easily back in October 2025.** Same mechanism in spirit (infinite-scroll, default-mode time-fill), but very different outcome. Why was Reddit easy to drop and short-form-video hard to drop? Same person, similar mechanism.

Candidate explanations (hypotheses, not answers):
- **Format effect:** short-form video may be structurally more compelling than text-feed scrolling (audiovisual, faster reward cycle, algorithmic precision).
- **State effect:** kicking Reddit in October 2025 happened from a different state than the current attempted-detox. Pre-burnout-trough then; deep in burnout-recovery now. Defences are different.
- **Prior-effect:** the "staunchly against" position may itself have been the defence. Once that identity-prior dropped (boredom? exhaustion? curiosity?), no replacement guardrail.
- **Compounding-effect:** short-form might have just had more time to embed. Reddit was bounded in scope (specific subs); reels are infinite and algorithmically personalised.

Not solving tonight. Hold as an open question — the W22 detox isn't tested on "why the hook formed" but might give data on "what removes it." Worth checking against this question post-detox.
