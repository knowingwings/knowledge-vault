## Daily Reflection (22:00, 5 min)

### What did I learn today that I didn't know yesterday?

**The hook may be shapeshifting, not disappearing.** Started reading on my phone today by picking up an unfinished book — and while reading is in the IN list of the detox bounds (chosen, intentional), doing it on the phone has a different shape than reading a physical book. Could be that when one route to easy stimulation is blocked, the brain finds an adjacent route within the rules. Worth holding as a hypothesis: detox protocols that target *behaviour* may displace rather than dissolve the underlying pull. Not a reason to drop the protocol — but a reason to watch for substitution patterns, not just compliance to the bounds.

**Software-first debugging when hardware-software stacks break.** Today's Reducer test got blocked because the Arduino wouldn't talk to the data collector on the laptop. 45 minutes of frustration before finding the cause: I'd run a kernel update without rebooting, so the USB driver state was stale. The deeper principle is *"check what changed most recently first"* — in this case, the recent change was software (the kernel update), so the software side would have been the right first place to look. The shorthand: **when hardware-software stacks break, check the software side first** — it's usually faster to investigate (logs, drivers, ports, dmesg) than to start probing hardware. Worth holding for future hardware bring-up sessions on Reducer + Algernon + rover arm.
