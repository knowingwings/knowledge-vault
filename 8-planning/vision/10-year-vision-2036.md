# 10-Year Vision (2036)

## Who I Want to Become

A space robotics pioneer who builds systems that physically operate in orbit. Not someone who works "in the space industry" from the ground, but someone whose creations are in space, doing real work. A polymath engineer with deep technical expertise and broad intellectual curiosity. Someone who has built meaningful ventures while maintaining genuine relationships, health, and a rich personal life.

## Life Domains

### Career / Professional

**By 2036, I am:**
- Founder/leader of Huginn Robotics (or successor venture) with operational hardware in orbit
- Recognised as a technical authority in space construction and orbital robotics
- Have personally contributed to systems that have been deployed to space
- Built and led engineering teams that ship real products

**The path:**
- 2026: Complete MSc at Bath with distinction, thesis in multi-robot space construction
- 2026-2028: Industry experience (Icarus Robotics or major player) building expertise and capital
- 2028-2030: Launch Huginn Robotics, initial product development
- 2030-2033: First space-qualified hardware, commercial contracts
- 2033-2036: Operational orbital presence, market leadership

### Knowledge / Intellectual

**By 2036, I am:**
- Deep expertise in space robotics, orbital mechanics, autonomous systems
- Conversational in French, German, and Spanish; functional in others as needed
- Broad understanding of philosophy, economics, and market dynamics
- Competent musician (piano, potentially saxophone)
- Known for ability to explain complex technical concepts clearly at any level

**Cross-domain connections I want to make:**
- Robotics + Economics: Understanding market dynamics for space industry development
- Philosophy + Engineering: Ethics of autonomous systems, decision-making frameworks
- Music + Technical work: Creative discipline transfer, pattern recognition

### Relationships / Community

**By 2036:**
- Strong family relationships maintained despite geographic distribution
- A network of collaborators, mentors, and friends across the space industry
- Relationships not sacrificed for career - regular presence with people who matter
- Part of communities in multiple locations (Guernsey, Europe, potentially orbital)

### Health / Physical

**By 2036:**
- Physically capable of passing commercial astronaut medical requirements
- Sustainable fitness routine that travels with me across locations
- Mental health maintained through balance, not burnout-and-recover cycles
- Energy and vitality to enjoy the lifestyle I'm building

### Financial

**Targets (as guideposts, not obsessions):**
- 2028: £200-250K personal savings (depending on investments), company funding secured
- 2032: £5M+ net worth (primarily equity in ventures)
- 2036: £20M+ net worth, financial independence achieved

**Philosophy:** Wealth as a tool for freedom and impact, not a scorecard. Enough to fund ventures, live well across multiple locations, and never have to compromise on interesting work for financial reasons.

## The Non-Negotiables

These MUST be true for 2036 to feel successful:

1. **Actual space hardware**: I have built things that have physically been to space and done useful work there. Not simulations, not ground support, not "space-adjacent" - real orbital operations.

2. **Work-life balance**: I have maintained meaningful relationships, physical health, and personal interests alongside career ambitions. Success achieved through burnout is not success.

3. **Ownership**: I own significant equity in what I build. Employee experience is a stepping stone, not the destination.

## What I'm Willing to Sacrifice

- **Geographic stability**: Will relocate for the right opportunities
- **Short-term income maximisation**: Will take lower salary for better equity/learning
- **Conventional career path**: Willing to take startup risk over corporate safety
- **Comfort**: Will work very hard during critical phases (but not indefinitely)
- **Immediate gratification**: Building for 10-year outcomes, not quarterly wins

## What I'm NOT Willing to Sacrifice

- Family relationships and presence at important moments
- Physical and mental health foundations
- Intellectual breadth and curiosity (not becoming a narrow specialist)
- Integrity and ethical standards in how I build and lead
- The actual goal (space hardware) for the appearance of success (prestigious but ground-bound roles)

## The Lifestyle Vision

**Multi-location presence by 2035:**
- Guernsey: Family base, business HQ, roots
- Switzerland: European operations, alpine lifestyle, ETHZ connections
- Nice/Mediterranean: Rest, creativity, warm climate option
- Orbital: Extended stays on operational platforms (the ultimate goal)

This isn't about luxury collection - it's about having the freedom to be where I need to be for work, relationships, and personal wellbeing without constraint.

## Review Triggers

Revisit this vision when:
- Major life changes (relationship, family, health)
- Significant career pivot points
- Annual review (December each year)
- When daily work feels disconnected from these goals
