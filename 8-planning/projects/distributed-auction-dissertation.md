# Distributed Auction Algorithm (BEng Dissertation)

Hub for the decentralised multi-robot task-allocation thread: the first-class BEng dissertation extending Zavlanos et al. (2008), its unvalidated core claims, and the planned validation and paper work.

**The work.** Extended the Zavlanos distributed-auction framework with time-weighted consensus and adaptive epsilon-scaling for decentralised task allocation across mobile manipulators. Clean results: convergence to balanced allocations, near-linear scaling, lower message complexity than centralised. Core guarantees (optimality, recovery, the adaptive mechanism) were not validated in the BEng. See [[founding-strategy-brief]] section 2.5.
