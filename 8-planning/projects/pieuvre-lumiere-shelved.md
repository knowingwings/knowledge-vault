# Pieuvre Lumière (PL) — shelved 2026-05-11

PL is a long-running concept (architectural models + 3D-printed designer lighting business, Guernsey-anchored). After a deep reassessment on 10-11/05/2026, **shelved** as a near-term venture. Note kept so future-me doesn't have to redo the work if revisiting.

**Reference doc:** `Pieuvre Lumière Business Plan - Realistic Version.pdf` (in this folder) — full 15-page plan from June 2025.

---

## What PL was supposed to be (per June 2025 plan)

- **Dual focus business** based in Guernsey, Channel Islands
  - Primary: architectural scale models for ~20 Guernsey architectural firms (B2B service)
  - Secondary: 3D-printed designer lighting (lampshades, interior products)
- **Phased growth** over 6 years: home workshop → established brand → potential staff
- **Equipment:** existing 3D print stack (Form 1 SLA needing stepper repair, Wanhao i3 clone needing recommissioning, Ender 5 Plus)
- **Startup capital:** £5,160
- **Year 1 revenue target:** £4,000-8,800 (net -£200 to +£3,000)
- **Year 4-6 revenue target:** £150,000-250,000 combined, 35-40% net margins
- **Mission:** "Revolutionise architectural visualisation through innovative physical models that seamlessly integrate interactive technology"
- **Name etymology:** "Pieuvre" (Guernesiais word for octopus, made famous by Victor Hugo) + "Lumière" (French for light) — local heritage + lighting specialty

## What was good about the old plan
- Financial realism (conservative revenue, low startup, break-even analysis)
- Risk assessment + contingency framing
- Strong brand etymology / origin story
- Lamp design collection concepts (Coastal/Architectural/Nature-Inspired)
- Solo-then-evaluate-hiring approach

## Why it was shelved (analysis from 10-11/05/2026)

1. **Architectural models was the wrong primary product:**
   - 20 firms in Guernsey = tiny TAM, even at 100% penetration
   - Service business — scales linearly with hours, not automatable
   - Sticky relationships with existing model-makers create switching costs
   - Doesn't build a consumer brand that supports the lighting line

2. **3D-printed designer lighting market is now saturated:**
   - The "6-8 years too late" observation (Tom's own): the technology novelty has eroded
   - Etsy is flooded with parametric Voronoi/spiral lamps
   - Winning now requires brand + design + photography discipline, not just engineering — different skillset than the founder has actively built
   - Compliance burden for full lamps (CE/UKCA, electrical safety) is real legal overhead, ~£500-2k for testing alone

3. **Capital reality has shifted:** the old plan assumed £5,160 startup. Current budget is ~£100. Architectural models + designer lighting at any meaningful scale requires more capital than is available.

4. **Founder-time reality has shifted:** the old plan assumed full-time founder. Tom is part-time on PL at best (alongside MSc → ISAM job runway phase).

5. **The two passions were conflated:** Tom genuinely loves **architecture** (separate side passion, similar shape to horology) AND **light** (separate obsession — cinematography, psychology of light, retail-lighting calmness). PL tried to bundle both. They don't need to share a venture.

6. **A different practice venture was identified:** **Knowingslab is now the short-term learning vehicle** for business/audience/brand skills — already exists, zero startup cost, identity-aligned with the ISAM trajectory. PL's "practice business" role is filled.

## What replaced PL in the short-term plan

- **Practice venture for business skills + audience-building:** knowingslab YouTube channel (already launching).
- **Short-term income (if needed):** part-time role search (e.g. The Raven applied 25/04).
- **Architecture and light as creative outlets:** parked — revisit independently as creative threads (not as one fused business) once the ISAM startup is rolling.

## Conditions under which PL might come back

Don't revisit unless several of these are true:
- Tom has substantial capital available (>£5k, ideally £10k+)
- Tom has time to actually run it (post-ISAM-job-phase, or as a partner/co-founder takes operational load)
- A genuinely differentiated positioning emerges (e.g. partnering with an architect or interior designer with established trade relationships)
- The "designer lighting" angle isn't the play — something more specific (commissioned architectural pieces for specific clients, gallery work, etc.)
- Or: a totally different product/service shape that uses the brand name + etymology but isn't the 2025 dual-focus plan
