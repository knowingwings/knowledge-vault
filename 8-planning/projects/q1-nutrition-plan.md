# Q1 2026 Nutrition Plan

## Overview

**Goal:** Balance performance (half marathon Mar 15), body composition, and budget (£30-50/week)

**Approach:** Simple guidelines, no detailed macro tracking. Focus on eating enough to fuel training while making smart budget choices.

---

## Daily Structure

| Meal | Timing | Focus |
|------|--------|-------|
| Breakfast | 06:30-07:00 (post-run on run days) | Carbs + protein for recovery/energy |
| Lunch | 12:00-13:00 | Balanced meal, protein + veg + carbs |
| Snack | 15:00-16:00 | Light, protein-focused |
| Dinner | 19:00-20:00 | Protein + veg, moderate carbs |
| Evening snack | Optional | If hungry after calisthenics |

---

## Run Day Nutrition

### Pre-Run (if needed, for long runs)
- Long runs (60+ min): Small snack 30-60 min before (banana, toast)
- Short runs (40-60 min): Can run fasted if preferred

### Post-Run (within 30-60 min)
- Prioritise protein + carbs
- Examples: Eggs on toast, porridge with protein, yoghurt with fruit and granola

### Long Run Days (Thu/Sun)
- Increase carbs day before and day of
- Extra snack in afternoon if needed
- Hydration: Extra 500ml-1L water

---

## Simple Guidelines (No Macro Counting)

### Protein at Every Meal
- Breakfast: Eggs, yoghurt, or protein in porridge
- Lunch: Chicken, tuna, beans, or eggs
- Dinner: Meat, fish, or plant protein
- Target: Palm-sized portion at each meal

### Carbs Around Training
- More on run days, especially long run days
- Less on rest days (Tue/Fri)
- Sources: Oats, rice, pasta, bread, potatoes

### Vegetables with Lunch and Dinner
- Half the plate = veg
- Frozen veg is budget-friendly and nutritious

### Hydration
- 2-3L water daily
- Extra 500ml-1L on run days
- Electrolytes for long runs if sweating heavily

## Budget Meal Ideas (£30-50/week)

### Budget Proteins
- Eggs (versatile, cheap)
- Chicken thighs (cheaper than breast)
- Tinned tuna/sardines
- Beans and lentils
- Frozen fish fillets
- Greek yoghurt (bulk tubs)

### Budget Carbs
- Oats (bulk bags)
- Rice (large bags)
- Pasta
- Potatoes
- Bread

### Budget Veg
- Frozen mixed veg
- Tinned tomatoes
- Carrots, onions (fresh, cheap)
- Cabbage, broccoli (when on offer)
- Bananas (cheap fruit)

### Sample Budget Day (~£4-5)
- **Breakfast:** Porridge with banana, honey (£0.50)
- **Lunch:** Rice + chicken thigh + frozen veg (£1.50)
- **Snack:** Yoghurt + handful of nuts (£0.70)
- **Dinner:** Pasta + tuna + tinned tomatoes + cheese (£1.50)
- **Evening:** Toast with peanut butter (£0.30)

### Batch Cooking Ideas
- Sunday: Cook big batch of rice + roast chicken thighs for Mon-Wed lunches
- Wednesday: Prep pasta sauce or curry for Thu-Sat

---

## Weekly Meal Prep Suggestions

### Sunday Evening (30-45 min)
- Cook batch of grains (rice or pasta)
- Prep protein for 3-4 days
- Chop veg for easy cooking
- Portion out snacks

### Wednesday Evening (20-30 min)
- Second batch cook for rest of week
- Check what needs using up

## What to Track

Keep it simple - just note in daily notes or a quick weekly check:

1. **Energy levels** - Are you fuelled for training? (1-5 scale)
2. **Recovery** - How do you feel day after long runs? (1-5 scale)
3. **Hunger** - Constantly hungry = probably undereating
4. **Weekly spend** - Quick total of food shopping

## Race Week Nutrition (Mar 9-15)

### Mon-Thu (Mar 9-12)
- Increase carbs slightly
- Stay hydrated
- Avoid new foods

### Fri-Sat (Mar 13-14)
- Carb loading - extra pasta/rice portions
- Reduce fibre slightly (less bloating)
- Extra hydration

### Race Morning (Mar 15)
- Familiar breakfast 2-3 hours before
- Light, carb-focused (toast, banana, porridge)
- Sip water, don't overdrink
- Maybe a coffee if that's normal routine

## Notes

- This is a starting framework - adjust based on how you feel
- If constantly tired or hungry, eat more (especially carbs on run days)
- Don't cut too hard on budget at expense of performance
- Ready meals are fine sometimes - just balance with home cooking
