## Part 5: Connections (15 min)

### Connection 1: "Do it before any decision branch" generalises

The shower-gate insight (the gap between "I should run" and actually running gets filled by other routine items) is a specific case of a broader principle: **anything with high activation energy benefits from being the first action of the day, before alternatives appear.** Once shower / food / phone / news inserts itself, the higher-friction thing loses.

Generalises to:
- Run-on-wake (the specific case identified today)
- App / cover letter writing (apps lost to morning routine multiple times this month)
- Bridge-work cold-starts (Reducer push, CAD sessions)
- Future: any creative work, language practice, etc.

The principle: **morning routine is a series of competing actions, and order is fate.** Whatever's first usually happens; whatever's last usually doesn't. So put the highest-leverage thing first, and put zero decisions between waking and starting it.

### Connection 2: Environment is load-bearing for state (not purely psychological)

Sofa sleeping working echoes the hardware-preference insight: **state isn't a pure-mind variable; it's substrate-and-environment.** Engineering minds tend to underweight environment as a state variable — *"I should be able to function anywhere, willpower is the lever"* — when actually the physical environment is doing significant work.

Evidence accumulating:
- **Bath flat quality factor** — flagged earlier as "quietly load-bearing for mental state"
- **London trip state improvement** — different environment correlated with better state during a hard period
- **Sofa-sleep state improvement** — same building, different room, measurable difference
- **Hardware-preference itself** — tangible / physical work feels different than data work in a way that affects engagement
- **Run-immediately-on-wake working when scheduled-run-with-friend doesn't** — environmental conditions (own pace, own time, own environment) outweigh social accountability for Tom specifically

**Implication for bigger decisions:** environment should be weighted in future career / location decisions (which ISAM job offer to take, where to base the startup, when to relocate). If state is environment-dependent, the right job in the wrong environment is worse than the slightly-wrong job in the right environment. Worth holding when role offers eventually arrive.
