## Part 5: Connections (15 min)

### Connection 1: State-vs-task distinction now visible

Running landed (5/3 runs, pace floor dropped) **on the same days** project layer collapsed. Pure substrate-deficit was the previous default explanation for the can't-start pattern — *"I'm too depleted, willpower can't substitute."* W21 falsifies the simple version of that: substrate was sufficient for running (which has its own activation energy and decision-cost) but not for project-layer work.

**The blocker is task-specific, not state-general.** Some specific cluster of properties separates "running" from "Reducer assembly":
- Running is repeatable, embodied, low-cognitive-load, well-defined start (put shoes on, go outside), and unambiguous done-condition (distance hit, watch logged)
- Reducer / apps / Algernon are project work: novel-load, ambiguous start, no clean done-condition, and crucially **face the H2 regret-comparison directly** (any project step is implicitly "the thing I should have been doing years ago")

**Implication:** the diagnostic stack narrows. Substrate matters but isn't the limiter. The detox will test the dopamine-hook hypothesis. If H1 fails to explain, H2 (regret-shame around tasks specifically) becomes primary — and that's a different intervention class (cognitive reframe / working-with-regret, not behavioural).

### Connection 2: Self-set deadlines don't bind, even with stated stakes

The 24/05 ship commit was W20's deliberate forcing-function fix — meant to address brake 5. **It failed.** Friends-told-in-advance never happened; self-set Sunday deadline didn't override the three-day project-stall. **Pattern same as W20:** coursework (external deadlines) shipped even on 0.5/5 days; bridge-work (no external structure) didn't.

**Generalises:** willingness to set a deadline ≠ ability of that deadline to bind. The binding force comes from external visibility / cost of failure being non-zero to someone else. Friends-told-in-advance was the structural fix designed to address this — and not executing it is the actual W21 failure on this brake, not the slip itself.

**Worth holding for future planning:** when committing to ship-by-X, the next step isn't "lock the date" but "lock who else knows and what their stake is." Without that, the commitment is performative, not structural.

### Connection 3: Diagnostic-first thinking (detox research catch)

The detox plan was about to be locked as a 72-96hr "dopamine reset" — a popular framing that the research showed is largely pop-psychology (receptor recalibration takes weeks-to-months, not days; 72hr is the *worst* point — peak discomfort, common quit-zone). **Catching this before running it** preserved diagnostic value: the detox is now framed as a behavioural diagnostic for H1, with falsifiable success criteria.

**Generalises:** for any planned intervention, ask "what hypothesis does this test, and how would I know if it failed?" before designing the protocol. Interventions designed to "solve" a problem on an uncertain mechanism waste the data. Interventions designed to *test* a hypothesis are useful regardless of outcome.

**Connects to the "so what?" writing layer memory** — same shape applied to interventions instead of writing: descriptive-default ("I'll do a detox to feel better") needs an analytical pass ("what would the detox tell me, and what would the result mean?"). The research catch forced that pass.
